About

Contributing to research and practice in Japanese–Egyptian education

"What does it take to transplant one of the world's most distinctive education systems into a completely different cultural context — and make it work?"

I am an Egyptian researcher and JICA Specialist at the Tokkatsu JICA project in Egypt, with a PhD in International & Comparative Education. I founded the Egyptian Kominkan social enterprise and the Global Kominkan initiative, and I am an EDU-port Japan certified project coordinator.

I collaborate with Okayama University, Tsukuba University, Shujitsu University, Toyo University, and the All Japan Kominkan Federation. My research interests include Tokkatsu, social education, teacher development, citizenship education, and peace education. I publish in Japanese, English, and Arabic.
